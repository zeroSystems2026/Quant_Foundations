# CS50 Week 00: Scratch

* Binary Logic: The limit of a computer's function is determined by 1s (on) and 0s (off) based on their location in a given number of bits, ranging from 8-, 16-, 32-, and 64-bits.
* ASCII: An 8-bit legend of 1s and 0s that translates to English letters and symbols, such as "A" and "!".
* Colors: Colors are shown by 3-byte mixtures of red, green, and blue that correspond, within an image, to different combinations of 1s and 0s.
* Algorithms: Algorithms are various ways to solve a problem with varying levels of speed and efficiency, like looking for the name Percy Jackson in a spreadsheet one box at a time versus discarding half of the data that doesn't fit the criteria.
* Pseudocode: Multiple lines of the standard English language and grammar that gives programmers easy-to-translate instructions to the programming language and allows the programmer to tell the computer how to solve a problem and what to do when it cannot solve a problem.
* Abstractions: The simplification of algorithmic-block coding by defining arguments, creating custom blocks, and removing the implementation details from the function block.
